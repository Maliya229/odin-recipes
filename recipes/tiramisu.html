Tiramisu Recipe

Description

Tiramisu is a classic Italian dessert made with layers of coffee-soaked ladyfinger biscuits and a rich, creamy mascarpone filling. The filling is prepared by blending mascarpone cheese with eggs, sugar, and a hint of vanilla, creating a smooth and indulgent texture. The ladyfingers are quickly dipped in strong coffee, then layered with the cream mixture and dusted with cocoa powder. After chilling for several hours, the flavors meld together beautifully, resulting in a soft, creamy, and coffee-infused dessert that’s perfect for any occasion.

Ingredients

  • 200g ladyfinger biscuits (savoiardi)
  • 250g mascarpone cheese
  • 1 cup strong brewed coffee (cooled)
  • 3 large eggs (separated)
  • 1/2 cup sugar
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 2 tablespoons cocoa powder (for dusting)
  • Optional: 2 tablespoons coffee liqueur

Steps

  1. Brew strong coffee and let it cool. Add coffee liqueur if using.
  2. Separate the eggs into yolks and whites.
  3. Whisk egg yolks with sugar until thick and pale.
  4. Add mascarpone cheese and vanilla extract, and mix until smooth.
  5. In another bowl, beat egg whites until stiff peaks form.
  6. Gently fold the egg whites into the mascarpone mixture.
  7. Quickly dip each ladyfinger into the coffee and arrange in a layer.
  8. Spread half of the cream mixture over the layer.
  9. Repeat with another layer of dipped ladyfingers and remaining cream.
  10. Dust the top with cocoa powder.
  11. Refrigerate for at least 4–6 hours before serving.
